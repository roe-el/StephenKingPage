Stephen King

Creator of Worlds

American author of horror, supernatural fiction, suspense, science fiction, and fantasy.

"Books are a uniquely portable magic." - Stephen King
ABOUT

Stephen King was born on September 21, 1947 in Portland, Maine. He and his brother were raised by their single mom after their father left when King was two years old. He fell in love with writing after finding The Lurker in the Shadows by H.P. Lovecraft in his attic, a book that had once belonged to his father. Since then he has written more then 200 short stories and his books have sold over more than 350 million copies. He has received numerous awards for his works and several of his stories have become major motion pictures.

NOTABLE WORKS

  • Carrie(1973)

    King's first novel about a teenage girl with psychic powers. He threw his early draft in the trash but his wife fished it out and encouraged him to finish it. It has become two feature films.

  • The Shining(1977)

    A story about an aspiring alcoholic writer who becomes the caretaker of a massive hotel in the Colorado Rockies. Supernatural forces push the writer to insanity. The novel was adapted into a film in 1980 directed by Stanley Kubrick.

  • Misery(1987)

    The novel tells the story of a writer who is famous for his romance novels who has a terrible car crash. He is rescued by a crazed fan who keeps him at her house and forces him to write a new book based on her ideas, torturing him physically and emotionally to get her way. In 1990, the novel was adapted into a film starring James Caan and Kathy Bates. Bates won the 1990 Best Actress Oscar for herr performance.

  • Rita Hayworth and Shawshank Redemption(1982)

    A novella by King that centers on a banker who is serving a double life sentence for murdering his wife and her lover. It was adapted for the screen in 1994 and won seven Academy Awards and is considered one of the best movies of all time.

  • The Dark Tower Series

    An eight-book epic that focuses on a lone gunslinger in an alternate-reality who quests after a mighty 'Dark Tower'. It was intended as a cross between J.R.R. Tolkiens's Middle-Earth and spaghetti era Westerns depicted by Clint Eastwood and Sergio Leone. King started the series in the late 1970's and the final book was published four decades later. It has been called King's magnum opus with a graphic novel spin-off series published by Marvel Comics and an upcomming feature film starring Idris Elba.
